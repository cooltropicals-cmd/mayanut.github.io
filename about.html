About The MayaNut Company

The MayaNut Company was founded in 2025 by August Kokus, who wanted to leave behind something new and unique in the world. He became fascinated by the idea of an ever-lasting creation — something that could adapt, evolve, and continue long after we’re gone. A living legacy. For him, that legacy is a tree. If it can feed people and fit into the future of agriculture, it has a reason to survive. Why not mayanut?

Our focus is working to establish South Florida’s first MayaNut™ plantation. Brosimum alicastrum — often called maya nut, ojoche, Ramón, or breadnut. It’s native to the tropical forests of Mesoamerica and the Caribbean, from Mexico and Guatemala through Belize and Honduras, into parts of Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Panama, and across to islands like Cuba and Jamaica, as well as northern regions of Colombia and Venezuela. In many areas, you’ll still find towering mayanut trees near ancient ceremonial sites, where they were likely planted and cared for by Indigenous communities.

Maya Nut in History

Humans in the Americas probably first encountered Brosimum alicastrum tens of thousands of years ago, as early hunter-gatherers and later settled Indigenous groups moved into tropical lowland forests. Over time, the tree became woven into local food traditions. In parts of the Maya region, its seeds were dried, roasted, ground into flour, or cooked into porridges and beverages. During times of drought or crop failure, mayanut helped people bridge the gap between harvests.

Today the species is still present in forests and shade agroforestry systems, but it’s underused in mainstream agriculture. We see that as a huge opportunity — both for farmers and for the tree itself.

What We’re Doing in South Florida

Today, the seed is still incredibly versatile. When roasted, it develops a warm, nutty flavor perfect for baked goods or as a caffeine-free coffee alternative. Ground MayaNut™ can be blended into drinks, smoothies, porridges, or used as a nutrient-rich flour. At The MayaNut Company, we are working to establish South Florida’s first MayaNut™ plantation, selecting strong genetics and learning how the trees respond to a new climate while introducing people to this forgotten superfood. Looking ahead, we’re developing the first line of MayaNut beverages in the United States. Our plan is to sell roasted, ground MayaNut™ for home brewing — smooth, rich, and naturally caffeine-free — along with pre-bottled MayaNut drinks. If all goes well, our bottled beverages will begin appearing in grocery stores around 2028, marking a new chapter for an ancient tree and a new agricultural future for South Florida.

Future Products

The seeds of Brosimum alicastrum are incredibly versatile. When roasted and ground, they can produce a rich, coffee-like beverage with their own unique character — naturally caffeine-free, but still full of flavor. We plan to offer ground MayaNut™ that people can brew at home, as well as ready-to-drink bottled mayanut beverages.

Our aim is to begin bringing these products to market — including grocery stores — around 2028. Between now and then, we’re focused on growing trees, refining recipes, and building something very special.
