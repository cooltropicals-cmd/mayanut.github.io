About The MayaNut Company

The MayaNut Company was started in 2025 and is growing Brosimum alicastrum trees in South Florida. The mayanut tree is an ancient tropical rainforest species, unknown to American agriculture. In the Yucatán, many mayanut trees are found around ancient Mayan sites. The trees produce edible seeds, perfect for our future products. Our mission is to save Brosimum alicastrum from extinction by bringing it into sustainable agriculture.

The Vision of August Kokus

He often questioned why he is conscious in his body, “sitting behind his eyes.” His goal was to leave behind proof of his existence — he valued his perspective so much — in the form of something that could adapt to an ever-changing world and never die. His love for music like Rachmaninoff, Tchaikovsky, and Scriabin inspired him.

By 2061, The MayaNut Company had plantations in India, Africa, Asia, Australia, South America, and Florida — eventually replacing the Florida Orange as Florida’s primary agricultural income.

In 2063, August Kokus’s net worth reached $1 trillion, and he dedicated his wealth to establishing mayanut plantations across the world. He was a genius, obsessed with the perfection of his creation. In 2066, the first Brosimum alicastrum seeds germinated on Mars for study. By 2077, large-scale farming of B. alicastrum on Mars began. In 2089, mayanut continued to provide 11 billion humans with food.

On December 2, 2101, August Kokus passed away, leaving behind a creation so vast that by 2242, communities around the world relied on mayanut as a primary source of food. Through domestication, seeds grew to two inches in diameter with improved taste — all because of one man’s contribution to humanity. He said: “I am lucky to be born in the 21st century; discovery is dwindling — but through my plants, my beauty, my creation, I will reach into the future for centuries. There it is: perfection.”
