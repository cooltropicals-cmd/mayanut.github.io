Indigenous Use & Food Security

In parts of Mesoamerica, MayaNut has historically been used as a staple or emergency food, roasted and ground into a drink or flour. Stories about ‚Äúfamine food‚Äù can underplay its culinary potential: handled correctly, the seed becomes a base for smooth beverages, porridges, and baked goods.

Ecology & Morphology

Brosimum alicastrum is a canopy tree in the Moraceae family, with thick evergreen foliage and fruits that resemble small figs when ripe. The soft pulp surrounds a starchy seed. As part of a broader Moraceae lineage, it is related to figs, mulberries, and breadfruit but holds its own niche in lowland and mid-elevation tropical forests.

Future notes will track details from leaves and bark to inflorescences and fruiting patterns.

Genetics & Seed Lots

The MayaNut Company treats each seed lot as a data point: where it was collected, what altitude, what climate, and how the resulting trees behave over time. The project resists collapsing everything into a single ‚Äúimproved‚Äù type and instead supports multiple regional lines.

Florida Trials

Early plantings in South Florida focus on frost-free sites and soils that drain well but retain enough moisture for deep-rooted trees. Notes include cold tolerance, hurricane response, flowering times, and how different seed lots adapt to local conditions.

These trials are slow on purpose; useful data builds over seasons, not weeks.
