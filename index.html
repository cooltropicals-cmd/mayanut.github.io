ABOUT THE MAYANUT COMPANY
Who is behind mayanut.com?

The MayaNut Company is a long-term project focused on MayaNut superfood from Brosimum alicastrum ‚Äì sometimes called ram√≥n ojoche guayumaro. The goal is to grow a superfood brand and, at the same time, safeguard the genetics of this underused tree.

Instead of chasing a single fast launch, the project is paced around tree time: planting, observing, and slowly learning how MayaNut behaves in different climates, including South Florida. That means every section of this page is designed to age well and stay useful for growers, researchers, and curious eaters.

On the consumer side, the vision includes ready-to-drink MayaNut beverages, roasted mayanut powder for recipes, and eventually bulk ingredients for chefs and food companies. Names & Common Synonyms

Brosimum alicastrum is known by many names: mayanut, breadnut, nuez de ram√≥n, ojoche, guayumaro, guaymoro, and regional spellings such as ox / oox in Maya-speaking areas. The goal here is to keep those names visible while making ‚ÄúMayaNut‚Äù an easy global label.

Indigenous Use & Food Security

In parts of Mesoamerica, MayaNut has historically been used as a staple or emergency food, roasted and ground into a drink or flour. Stories about ‚Äúfamine food‚Äù sometimes underplay its real culinary potential: when handled correctly, the seed can become a base for smooth beverages, porridges, and baked goods.

Long-term goal: document how MayaNut can contribute to climate-resilient diets today, without rewriting the past or overstating claims.

Ecology & Morphology

Brosimum alicastrum is a canopy tree in the Moraceae family, with thick evergreen foliage and fruits that resemble small figs when ripe. The soft pulp surrounds a starchy seed. Within Moraceae, it sits beside figs, mulberries, and breadfruit but fills its own niche in lowland and mid-elevation tropical forests.

Research here will track not just fruit and seed, but canopy form, rooting behavior, and hurricane response where relevant.

Genetics & Seed Lots

The MayaNut Company treats each seed lot as a data point: where it was collected, what altitude, what climate, and how the resulting trees behave over time. The long view is to create living collections that preserve regional lines instead of collapsing everything into a single anonymous ‚Äúimproved‚Äù type.

Future updates will summarize traits ‚Äì growth speed, canopy height, fruiting patterns ‚Äì without pretending to replace formal genetic studies.

Florida Trials

Early plantings in South Florida focus on frost-free sites and soils that drain well but still hold enough moisture for deep-rooted trees. Notes from these trials ‚Äì including cold snaps, wind events, and flowering windows ‚Äì will appear here once there is enough data to be useful.

The idea is to replace speculation with actual, multi-year observations.

What is MayaNut?

‚ÄúMayaNut‚Äù is our modern name for the edible seed of Brosimum alicastrum, a tropical canopy tree in the Moraceae family. The roasted seed can be ground into a powder for drinks, tortillas, flatbreads, and baked goods. It is naturally caffeine-free and fits neatly into today‚Äôs world of superfood beverages and clean label ingredients.

Is this the same as ram√≥n / ojoche / guaimaro?

Yes ‚Äì in many cases those names refer to the same tree. Across its native range people say ram√≥n, ojoche, guayumaro, ox / oox, guaimaro, and more. The MayaNut Company uses ‚ÄúMayaNut‚Äù as an easy, global name, but we respect and reference these local names whenever we talk about the tree‚Äôs history and cultural use.

When will products be for sale?

The first commercial MayaNut powder and ready-to-drink concepts are planned for sometime around the late 2020s. Trees must be planted, grown, and harvested carefully. When products are ready, the store and cart icon on this site will move from ‚Äúcoming soon‚Äù to active ordering.

What is the long-term directive of this website?

This site exists to help ensure the survival of Brosimum alicastrum at an academic and genetic level. That means collecting seeds from many regions, tracking how different lines behave, and sharing a clear, public record of what we learn. mayanut.com should be useful to growers, researchers, and curious eaters ‚Äì not just today, but decades from now.

In short: build a superfood company that also functions as a living archive and reforestation engine for MayaNut.

Can I grow MayaNut myself?

In tropical and frost-free subtropical climates, it is possible to grow Brosimum alicastrum, especially where winter temperatures rarely drop below freezing.
