About MayaNut™

MayaNut™ is a delicious tree seed of Brosimum alicastrum, a tall rainforest tree capable of producing many hundreds of seeds. In Florida, due to moderate rainfall patterns, trees only fruit once in March and April, producing sweet fig-like fruit which is chewy and has a syrupy, slightly tangy taste. The fruit, which is elusively talked about, is so delicious and best eaten when red, yet has a very short shelf-life — only fresh fruit should be eaten.

The seed inside, however, is very nutritious. It can be roasted and ground into a powder used in our signature MayaNut™ Beverage. It can also be ground and mixed with water to make delicious bread or cookies. Nevertheless, Brosimum alicastrum continues to be an underutilized species, which is why we are building the first MayaNut™ plantation in the United States.

Our Work in South Florida

MayaNut™ seems to grow reliably only up to about USDA Zone 9. Temperatures below 50°F can damage new growth, temperatures under 38°F can cause defoliation, and a hard freeze can kill mature trees. That makes South Florida and a few pockets of southern Texas the only places in the continental United States that can support viable agricultural plantings.

South Florida, with its subtropical climate, is especially promising. Our goal is to establish Florida’s first dedicated MayaNut™ plantation and refine how the trees fit into diversified farms — alongside fruit trees, pasture, and native plants.
