About MayaNut™

MayaNut™ is a delicious tree seed of Brosimum alicastrum, a tall rainforest tree capable of producing many hundreds of seeds. In Florida, due to moderate rainfall patterns, trees only fruit once in March and April, producing sweet fig-like fruit which is chewy and has a syrupy, slightly tangy taste. The fruit — which is elusively talked about — is so delicious and best eaten when red, yet has a very short shelf-life; only fresh fruit should be eaten. The seed inside, however, is very nutritious. It can be roasted and ground into a powder used in our signature MayaNut™ Beverage. It can also be ground and mixed with water to make delicious bread or cookies. Nevertheless, Brosimum alicastrum continues to be an underutilized species, which is why we are the first MayaNut™ plantation in the United States.

August Kokus is counting on that: a unique, new creation… MayaNut™.

Our Work in South Florida

MayaNut™ seems to only grow up to zone 9 in the United States. Temperatures below 50°F can begin to damage new growth, temperatures under 38°F can cause defoliation, and temperatures under 32°F can kill mature MayaNut™ trees. South Florida and parts of southern Texas are the only places in the continental United States that could support viable agricultural projects. South Florida, with its subtropical environment, appears to be the most ideal location. Florida’s first MayaNut™ plantation is set to open in January 2027.
