About MayaNut™

MayaNut™ is a delicious tree seed of Brosimum alicastrum, a tall rainforest tree capable of producing many hundreds of seeds. In Florida, due to moderate rainfall patterns, trees only fruit once in March and April, producing sweet fig-like fruit that is chewy with a syrupy, slightly tangy taste. The fruit, which is elusively talked about, is best eaten fresh when red — it has a very short shelf life.

The seed inside, however, is very nutritious. It can be roasted and ground into a powder used in our signature MayaNut™ Beverage. It can also be ground and mixed with water to make delicious bread or cookies. Nevertheless, Brosimum alicastrum continues to be an under-utilized species, which is why we are developing the first MayaNut™ plantation in the United States.

Our Work in South Florida

MayaNut™ appears to grow reliably only up to USDA zone 9 in the United States. Temperatures below 50°F can begin to damage new growth, temperatures under 38°F can cause defoliation, and sustained freezes under 32°F can kill mature trees. That leaves South Florida and parts of southern Texas as the only places in the continental U.S. suitable for long-term agricultural projects.

South Florida’s subtropical climate makes it especially well suited, which is why Florida’s first dedicated MayaNut™ plantation is scheduled to open in January 2027.
