Common Names & Synonyms

Brosimum alicastrum is known by many common names across its range. In English and Spanish it is often called maya nut or nuez de ram√≥n. Other names include breadnut, ojoche, ram√≥n, guayumaro, oox or ox. In parts of Colombia and Venezuela, local names emphasize its role as a shade and food tree. Botanically, two subspecies are recognized, though regional variation is far broader than two categories suggest.

Names matter ‚Äî they preserve history and reveal how people interacted with the tree long before it was a ‚Äúsuperfood.‚Äù

Indigenous Use & Food Security

In parts of Mesoamerica, MayaNut has historically been used as a staple or emergency food, roasted and ground into a drink or flour. Stories about ‚Äúfamine food‚Äù sometimes underplay its real culinary potential: handled correctly, the seed becomes a base for smooth beverages, porridges, and baked goods.

Ecology & Morphology

Brosimum alicastrum is a canopy tree in the Moraceae family, with thick evergreen foliage and fruits that resemble small figs when ripe. The soft pulp surrounds a starchy seed. As part of the broader Moraceae lineage, the tree is related to figs, mulberries, and breadfruit, but it occupies its own niche in lowland and mid-elevation tropical forests where it stabilizes soils and stores carbon.
