ABOUT

About The MayaNut Company

The MayaNut Company was created to bring the ancient Brosimum alicastrum tree back into the modern world. Also known as the Maya nut or Ojoche, this species has been used for thousands of years to support nutrition, agriculture, and sustainable food systems. Our mission is to plant, grow, and process the world’s cleanest Maya nut products using a model that respects nature, community and long-term sustainability.

Our first planned plantation is being established in Florida using carefully selected seeds from high-producing Brosimum alicastrum trees. Each planting location is designed with optimal spacing, irrigation, soil enrichment and sun exposure to maximize both flavor and yield. This plantation will form the foundation of a future line of beverages, powders, and flours.

Our vision is long-term: a world where the Maya nut becomes a staple, grown sustainably, harvested ethically, and enjoyed globally — all while supporting ecosystems and communities.

RESEARCH

Brosimum alicastrum – Research & Science

The Maya nut has been recognized for its nutritional density and sustainability. The pulp and seed contain plant-based protein, calcium, fiber, iron, potassium, and antioxidants. Research in agroforestry also shows that Brosimum alicastrum trees enrich their surrounding ecosystems by improving soil structure, promoting biodiversity, and providing natural shade in tropical climates.

Our research includes optimal germination methods, irrigation cycles, soil types, planting distances, male-to-female tree ratios, growth timelines, and natural pest solutions such as kaolin clay leaf coatings. We are combining modern horticultural knowledge with ancient cultivation practices.

The future goal is to publish our long-term findings, documenting the complete growth cycle, flavor profiles, harvest times, and ideal processing methods to produce world-class Maya nut beverages and flours.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is maya nut?

Maya nut is the edible seed of the tropical tree Brosimum alicastrum. The seeds can be dried, roasted and ground into a powder for beverages, baking, or traditional foods.

Where are your trees grown?

Our first planned plantations are in Florida, focusing on warm subtropical locations with the right soil, irrigation and protection for long-term Brosimum alicastrum growth.

When will products be available?

Our first trees are growing now. Once production is stable and quality is perfected, we plan to introduce beverages and powders later in the decade.

Can I collaborate or partner?

We welcome interest from growers, researchers and future distributors. See the Contact section below for how to reach us.
